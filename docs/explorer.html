UN M49 Standard Country or Area Codes

The foundational UN classification system published by the UN Statistics Division (UNSD). Covers all 248 UN statistical countries and areas — including territories and dependencies that may not be members of the UN or classified by the World Bank or OECD. Provides the global geoscheme (global → region → sub-region → intermediate region), plus three special designations: LDC (Least Developed Countries), LLDC (Landlocked Developing Countries), and SIDS (Small Island Developing States).

World Bank Country & Lending Groups

The World Bank classifies 215 economies by income level and lending eligibility. 33 UN statistical territories are not included (e.g. French overseas territories, Western Sahara, British Indian Ocean Territory) — this explains why the World Bank count for Africa is 54, not 60. Income levels are updated annually each July using Atlas GNI per capita data. Lending type indicates eligibility for IDA (concessional) or IBRD financing.

OECD DAC List of ODA Recipients

The OECD Development Assistance Committee (DAC) publishes the official list of countries eligible to receive Official Development Assistance (ODA). The current list covers — countries and territories, grouped by DAC category which roughly tracks World Bank income levels. Only countries on this list can be reported as ODA recipients in donor statistics. High-income countries are not on this list.

World Bank — Fragile and Conflict-affected Situations (FCS)

The World Bank publishes an annual list of countries classified as Fragile and Conflict-affected Situations (FCS). Countries are grouped into two categories: Conflict (active conflict with significant impact on governance and service delivery) and Institutional and Social Fragility (weak institutions, social cohesion, or high vulnerability without active conflict). The list informs IDA resource allocation and World Bank operational policies.

UN SDG Geographic Regions

The UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) monitoring framework uses its own geographic grouping system, which is different from the UN M49 geoscheme. SDG regions are designed for SDG indicator aggregation and include overlapping groups — for example, a country can appear in both "Eastern Africa" and "Sub-Saharan Africa" and "LDC Africa" simultaneously. There are — SDG groups covering all 248 UN statistical areas.
